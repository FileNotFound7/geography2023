About Rainforests

Rainforests are an absolutely crucial part of the earth's ecosystem. They provide clean air and support water cycles. Although they only cover 0.3% of Australia, they are the homes of half of native flora and a third of all native fauna thrive in rainforests.

Here are some other things that rainforests do:

• Rainforests help to prevent flooding

• The high amount of roots in rainforests help to prevent siltation and soil loss

• Around 7,000 medicines prescribed by doctors are derived from plants

• A rainforest's heavy canopy support many organisms
