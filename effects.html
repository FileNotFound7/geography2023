Changing Rainforests

Short Term Effects of Changing Rainforests

Some of these may just be effects of climate change because all of the causes impact climate change

Impacts of Overexploitation

Short Term Impacts

• Overexploitation of rainforests for medicines, food and fibres can cause disturbance of other plants and animals, which can throw out an ecosystem's balance.
• Some types of overexploitation like mining can leave behind toxic or hazardous waste which can be carried into rainforest ecosystems, poisoning and harming flora and fauna.

Long Term Impacts

• Overexploitation can deplete natural resources, potentially stopping animals and plants from accessing nutrients that they require to survive.
• Overexploitation may harm certain species, causing extinction and a decrease in overall biodiversity.

Impacts of Deforestation

Short Term Impacts

• Deforestation of rainforest areas can cause great harm to species of animals and plants, while also inhibiting the filtration of air, making the greenhouse effect even worse.
• Deforestation can cause a lower amount of carbon dioxide being filtered into oxygen which then exacerbates the greenhouse effect, raising temperature and making disasters like bushfires more likely.

Long Term Impacts

• Less rainforests can lead to a loss of biodiversity because some species may be left with nowhere else to go.
• Deforestation can be a cause of native animals going extinct and being replaced by invasive ones

Impacts of Pollution

Short Term Impacts

• Pollution from things like factories heavily impact climate change, which may lead to higher or lower humidity in a rainforests which can disturd the balance required for plants and other organisms.
• Climate change may cause more extreme temperatures which will cause it the rainforest to be uninhabitable to animals who otherwise could have lived in the rainforest happily.

Long Term Impacts

• Climate change caused by pollution can disrupt an environment's ability to stave off diseases and pathogens, causing plagues and overall lower efficiency.
• Pollution may cause worsening climate change, which can cause more frequent and severe weather events and storms that can permanently damage trees and the areas around them.
